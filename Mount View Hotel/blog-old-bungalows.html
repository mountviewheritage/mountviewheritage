The Timeless Charm of Panchgani's Old Bungalows

Scattered across the hills of Panchgani are remnants of a bygone era—the old bungalows. These structures tell stories of the British colonial period, Parsi settlers, and families who sought the cool retreat of the Sahyadris. Distinctive for their sturdy stone walls, sloping roofs, and extensive gardens, they are architectural gems hiding in plain sight.

Visiting or staying in one of these bungalows is like stepping into a living museum. You notice the attention to detail in the woodwork, the spaciousness of the rooms designed for cross-ventilation, and the way the house sits respectfully within the landscape rather than dominating it.

Preserving these bungalows is crucial not just for history's sake but for maintaining the unique character of the town. They remind us of sustainable building practices that prioritized locally sourced materials and harmony with nature.

For photo enthusiasts and history buffs, a walk through the older parts of town to spot these structures is highly recommended. Each gate and garden wall has a whisper of the past, waiting to be heard by those willing to listen.
